🔀 Master Algorithm Flowchart

🫀 Initial Assessment
Confirm: NE ≥ 0.1 mcg/kg/min for MAP ≥ 65 + Lactate ≥ 2 mmol/L
Measure baseline CRT (glass slide, 15s press, chronometer)
CRT ≤ 3 seconds?
✓ CRT ≤ 3s (Normal)
Normal Monitoring
Reassess CRT every 1 hour
If CRT deteriorates > 3s → re-enter Tier 1
✕ CRT > 3s (Abnormal)
Enter TIER 1
Pulse Pressure → Fluid Responsiveness → Fluid Bolus

⬛ TIER 1 — Hemodynamic Optimisation

Step 1: Pulse Pressure Assessment
Enter SBP + DBP → calculate PP and MAP
PP = SBP − DBP  |  MAP = (SBP + 2×DBP) / 3
PP ≤ 30 mmHg or DBP < 30 mmHg?
⚠ YES — Low PP/DBP
Uptitrate Norepinephrine
Target: DBP ≥ 30 mmHg, PP > 30 mmHg
Increase NE by 0.05–0.1 mcg/kg/min increments
Confirm before proceeding
✓ PP > 30 & DBP ≥ 30
Vasopressor Adequate
Proceed directly to FR assessment
Step 2: Fluid Responsiveness (FR) Assessment
Methods (in order of preference):
1. PLR + VTI (≥10% increase)  Preferred
2. PLR clinical assessment
3. End-expiratory occlusion + VTI (ventilated only)
4. PPV/SVV (>13%, fully ventilated + regular rhythm)
⚠ FR is MANDATORY before any fluid
FR Positive
100 mL Crystalloid Bolus
Infuse over 10 min
Safety checks: SpO₂, rales, CVP
Re-check FR → repeat bolus if still FR+ (max 3)
Stop if: worsening O₂, new rales, FR−
FR Negative / Safety Issue
No Fluid → CRT Reassess
Skip bolus, proceed to CRT reassessment
🔄 CRT Reassessment (after every intervention)
CRT ≤ 3s → Normal Monitoring (hourly)  |  CRT > 3s → Next intervention (smart routing based on what's been done)

⬛ TIER 2 — Phenotype-Based Interventions

Only ~35% of patients need Tier 2. Entered when CRT remains abnormal after Tier 1.

Step 1: Bedside Echocardiography
LV: FAC < 40% AND VTI < 14 cm → LV dysfunction
RV: RV/LV > 1 AND CVP > 8 mmHg → RV failure
Neither: No specific cardiac phenotype

LV Dobutamine
Start 5 mcg/kg/min
Titrate q15-30min to max 20
Stop if: arrhythmia, HR>120
Reassess CRT at 1 hr

Avoid fluid loading

Optimise ventilator, HR
No Cardiac Dx
Skip to Tier 2 FR
Repeat fluid responsiveness

🫁 Echo Decision Criteria

MeasurementThresholdMeaningAction
FAC< 40%LV systolic dysfunction→ Dobutamine (if VTI also low)
Aortic VTI< 14 cmLow stroke volume
RV/LV ratio> 1RV dilatation→ RV management (if CVP also high)
CVP> 8 mmHgElevated RV filling pressure

Both criteria within each phenotype must be met. If both LV and RV criteria met simultaneously, LV takes priority.

🛑 Safety Stops

Fluid administration is withheld when:

  • Worsening oxygenation — SpO₂ decreasing or FiO₂ increasing
  • New pulmonary rales — bilateral crackles on auscultation
  • FR Negative — no increase in cardiac output after test
  • Max boluses reached — 3 boluses before mandatory reassessment
  • RV failure identified — echo shows RV/LV >1 + CVP >8 → NO further fluids
